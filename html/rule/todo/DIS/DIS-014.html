DIS-014: StrictMark \* escape and 3-dash --- ruler unimplemented

StrictMark lists \* escapes as an inline element (second-highest precedence) and ---/--- /---- as rulers, but the MKDT lexer implements neither: a backslash is plain punctuation and only a four-dash ---- is recognised as a ruler. The goal is conformance with the two documented StrictMark constructs. See StrictMark, Mark.

Issues

Two distinct lexer gaps, both confirmed by running build/bin/mark.

  • \* escape: dog/tok/MKDT.c.rl has no backslash rule and MARKE (mark/MARKE.c.rl) maps only & < > ". Input a \* b *strong* c renders a \* b <strong>strong</strong> c — the backslash is emitted literally and fails to suppress the following strong span (StrictMark §"Inline markup").
  • --- ruler: dog/tok/MKDT.c:108-124 (MKDTHRule) requires exactly four dashes; a --- line renders as a paragraph, no <hr> (StrictMark §"Block markup" — "two shorter cases: a 3-backtick code fence and a 3-dash --- ruler").

Blockers

None; both live in the MKDT ragel/line lexer (regenerate with ragel -C MKDT.c.rl -o MKDT.rl.c -L).

Planned

Add the escape rule and the 3-dash ruler, with repro cases first.

  • Inline: a \<punct> escape that emits the literal char and cancels its bracketing role.
  • Block: accept a 3-dash --- (and --- ) line as a ruler alongside ----.
  • Add test/MARK.c cases for \* suppression and a --- <hr>.
