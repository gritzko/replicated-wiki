JS-002 CRIT: file.write(string) / io.log(string) heap corruption — invalid free + uninitialised write

JABCutf8CopyStringValue (js/utf8.cpp:63-71) advances the slice HEAD past the written bytes, so the caller writes the uninitialised tail and frees a non-allocation-base pointer. Flagged by 5 of 7 review lenses (very high confidence). Method: Issues.

Input

Context

  • $u8alloc(into,max) sets into={base, base+max} (abc/Sx.h:491: head=base),

then JABCutf8CopyStringValue does into[0]+=fact — advancing the HEAD past the copied bytes, leaving into={base+fact, base+max}.

  • Caller JABCioFileWrite (io.cpp:111) does write(fd, ta[0], $len(ta)) → it

writes from base+fact for max-fact bytes (the UNINITIALISED tail), not the string. Then $u8free (io.cpp:124) frees base+fact — a non-base pointer → heap corruption / invalid free.

  • fact includes the NUL terminator (off-by-one in the length too).
  • Severity unchanged by the WITH_JS gate: file.write/io.log are the live,

wired binding entry points, not the dead rdx path.

Goals

  • file.write("hello") writes exactly hello (5 bytes) and frees the real base.

Constraints

  • The copy helper must leave into as a DATA slice (term=base+fact-1, head

stays at base); the caller writes [base, len) and frees base. Use the idiomatic ABC slice term-set, not a head advance.

WIP

Design decisions

  • (open) Fix in JABCutf8CopyStringValue: set the slice TERM to the byte count

(excluding NUL), keep HEAD at base. Audit every caller for the head==base assumption.
