POST-010: no-op file:// re-push prints a stray FILEFAIL while reporting success

A no-op re-push over the local-exec file:// edge (nothing new to send) prints a stray FILEFAIL from wpush_send_pack_interleaved (keeper/WIRECLI.c:1492) even though the push still reports success — a benign EPIPE/POLLHUP race when the peer closes its end early on an empty/already-current pack. Local-exec only; not seen on the ssh path. Split out of [POST-008]. Cosmetic but noisy — a spurious error line on a successful no-op erodes trust in the output. See POST, CLAUDE.

Issues

An empty/no-op pack send races the peer's early close.

  • wpush_send_pack_interleaved (keeper/WIRECLI.c:1492) reports FILEFAIL on EPIPE/POLLHUP when the peer has nothing to receive and closes first; the overall push still returns success.
  • Only on the file:// local-exec transport; the ssh path doesn't surface it.
  • Repro: be post file://<abs> twice — the second (no-op) prints a stray FILEFAIL then "pushed"/success.

Blockers

None. Localized to the push send loop's error handling.

Planned

Treat an early peer close on an empty/current pack as a clean no-op.

  • Repro first (CLAUDE §17): a test asserting a second (no-op) file:// push prints no FILEFAIL and exits 0.
  • In wpush_send_pack_interleaved, swallow EPIPE/POLLHUP when the pack is empty / the peer is already current; only report a real send failure.
